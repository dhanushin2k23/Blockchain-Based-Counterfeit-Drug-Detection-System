Ensuring the authenticity of medicines for a healthier world.

Safe Medicines, Safe Lives.

1 in 10 medical products in low-income countries is substandard.
The counterfeit drug market is worth $200 billion annually.
Fake medicines contribute to nearly 500,000 deaths yearly.

  1. Steady Rise (2011-2013): A moderate increase in counterfeit drug cases, with growth rates of 2% (2012) and 8% (2013)
  2. Stagnation (2014): A -1%
  3. Significant Spike (2015-2019): Decline, indicating a temporary drop in reported cases. 2015 saw a sharp 27% rise, suggesting an increase in counterfeit drug production and distribution. 2016-2019 experienced continuous growth, with rates ranging from 5% to 18%.
  4. Pandemic Impact (2020): A -17% decline, likely due to global lockdowns, disrupted supply chains, and increased regulation.
  5. Post-Pandemic Surge (2021-2023): 2021 rebounded with a 27% increase—the highest in recent years—possibly due to the return of illicit drug networks. 2022 and 2023 saw further increases of 9% and 4%, respectively, indicating a persistent problem.
